# CUSTOMER RESPONSE TEMPLATES
**Communication Scripts for Help Desk Agents**

## PURPOSE
These templates provide professional, effective language for common support scenarios. Customize them to match your natural speaking style while maintaining professionalism.

**Use these to:**
- Ensure consistent customer experience
- Handle difficult situations professionally
- Communicate technical information clearly
- Manage customer expectations effectively

## üìû CALL OPENING SCRIPTS

### Standard Greeting
```
"Thank you for contacting [Company Name] Support, this is [Your Name].
Can I start with your ship name or call sign?"
```

### After Hours/High Volume
```
"Thank you for contacting [Company Name] Support, this is [Your Name].
I apologize for the wait time. Can I have your ship name so I can help you?"
```

### Return/Repeat Caller
```
"Thanks for calling back, [Captain Name]. This is [Your Name].
I see we spoke earlier about [issue]. How can I help you further?"
```

### Acknowledging Emergency
```
"I understand this is an emergency situation. I'm going to help you

### Staying Calm Under Pressure
```
"I know this situation is stressful, but we're going to work through
this together step by step. I need you to stay with me and follow
my instructions carefully. Can you do that?"
```

### Emergency Escalation
```
"I'm bringing in our emergency response team right now to assist.
They'll be on the line in less than 30 seconds. While we wait,

```
"This is what I need you to do right now:
1. [First action]
2. [Second action]
3. [Third action]

Can you start with step 1 while I stay on the line?"
```

### Beginning Troubleshooting
```
"Based on what you've described, I have a few diagnostic steps we can
try that should resolve this. This should take about [X] minutes.

```
"I'm going to walk you through this step by step. Think of it like
[simple analogy]. First, I need you to..."

```
"Let's start with the basics and rule out the common causes.

```

```
"Let me make sure I'm explaining this clearly. Can you tell me back
what you're going to do?"
```

### Step-by-Step Instructions
```
"Okay, here's step 1: [Action]
Take your time, and let me know when you've completed that step."

[After confirmation]
"Perfect. Now step 2: [Action]..."
```

### When Customer Has Already Tried Basic Steps
```
"I appreciate that you've already tried those steps. That actually
helps narrow down the problem. Let's move to the next level of
diagnostics..."
```

## ‚è±Ô∏è SETTING EXPECTATIONS

### Time Estimates
```
"Based on what you've described, troubleshooting this typically takes
[X-Y] minutes. I'll update you every [X] minutes on our progress.
Does that timeline work for you?"
```

### When Solution Will Take Time
```
"This fix is going to take approximately [time] to complete. During
that time, [what will happen]. Is there anything urgent you need to
do before we begin?"
```

### When You Need to Research
```
"That's a great question, and I want to give you accurate information.
Let me check our technical documentation. This will take about
2 minutes. Can you hold, or would you prefer I call you back?"
```

### Multi-Step Process
```
"Resolving this will involve [number] steps:
1. [Step name] - [time]
2. [Step name] - [time]
3. [Step name] - [time]

We can do this all now, or break it up if you need to. What works best?"
```

```

## üîÄ ESCALATION SCENARIOS

### Escalating to Level 2
```
"Based on what we've found, this issue requires our specialist team's
expertise. I'm going to connect you with [Specialist Type] who has
deeper knowledge of this system. I'll give them all the information
we've gathered so you don't have to repeat yourself. Is now a good time
for that transfer?"
```

### Escalating to Emergency Services
```
"Given the severity of this situation, I'm engaging our emergency
response team. They can coordinate [service needed]. This is the right
call to ensure your safety."
```

### Cannot Resolve - Need Ship Return
```
"After our troubleshooting, I believe this issue requires hands-on
service. I'd like to arrange for you to bring the ship to the nearest
service port at [location]. We can expedite the repair because of
[reason]. Would you like me to schedule that?"
```

### Need Parts/Supplies
```
"To complete this repair, you'll need [part/supply]. You can get this
at [location], or we can ship it to your current location which would
take [time]. What works better for you?"
```

## üò§ DIFFICULT SITUATIONS

### Angry Customer - Acknowledgment
```
"I completely understand your frustration. This situation [is/would be]
frustrating for anyone. I want to help you resolve this as quickly as
possible. Let's focus on [next step]."
```

### Angry Customer - De-escalation
```
"I hear that you're upset, and you have every right to be. What I'd like
to do is [solution]. Can we work on that together?"
```

### Customer Blaming You/Company
```
"I understand you feel that way. My priority right now is getting your
ship working properly. Can we focus on solving the problem, and then I'll
make sure your feedback about [issue] reaches the right people?"
```

### Customer Wants Things You Can't Provide
```
"I wish I could do that for you, but [reason]. What I can do is [alternative].
Would that help?"
```

### Customer Asking for Manager
```
"I'd be happy to get my supervisor involved. Before I do, let me make sure
I understand what you need so I can brief them: [summarize]. Is that accurate?"
```

## ‚ùå WHEN YOU DON'T KNOW THE ANSWER

### Don't Know - Need to Research
```
"That's an excellent question. I want to give you the right answer rather
than guess. Let me check our technical resources. This will take about
[X] minutes. I'll stay on the line."
```

### Don't Know - Need Expert
```
"That's outside my area of expertise, but I know who can answer that
definitively. Let me connect you with [specialist] who handles these
specific situations."
```

### Situation Never Seen Before
```
"I'll be honest - I haven't encountered this specific situation before.
That tells me we should get our specialist team involved right away
rather than trying things that might not work. Let me escalate this
for you."
```

## üö´ LIMITATIONS & BAD NEWS

### Outside Warranty Coverage
```
"I've checked your warranty status, and unfortunately this issue falls
outside coverage because [reason]. However, I can still help you troubleshoot
and provide options for repair. Would you like to proceed?"
```

### Will Cost Money to Fix
```
"Based on our diagnosis, this repair will require [service] which costs
approximately [amount]. Before we proceed, I wanted to make sure you're
comfortable with that cost. Would you like to move forward?"
```

### Cannot Fix Remotely
```
"After our troubleshooting, this isn't something we can resolve remotely.
The ship will need to come to a service facility for [reason]. I know
that's not ideal, but it's necessary to properly fix this issue."
```

### Long Lead Time
```
"I want to set proper expectations: this repair/part will take [timeframe]
because [reason]. I know that's longer than you'd like. In the meantime,
[workaround if any]."
```

### User Error (Tactful)
```
"I can see why that happened - that's a common misunderstanding of how
[system] works. The good news is there's no damage, and I can show you
the correct way to do this so it doesn't happen again."
```

```
"I'm implementing a temporary fix that should get you operational.
However, you'll need to monitor [system] and [what to watch for].
If you see [warning signs], contact us immediately."

## üìù CALL CLOSING SCRIPTS

### Standard Close - Issue Resolved
```
"Is there anything else I can help you with today?"

[If NO]
"Alright, you have my contact information if anything else comes up.
Have a safe journey, and thank you for contacting [Company Name] Support."
```

### Close with Follow-Up
```
"I've created a ticket for this issue: [ticket number]. I'll personally
follow up with you in [timeframe] to make sure everything is still
working properly. Is [contact method] the best way to reach you?"
```

### Close with Scheduled Callback
```
"I'm scheduling a callback for [date/time] to [reason]. Does that work
for your schedule? And what's the best frequency to reach you on?"
```

### Close with Escalation Pending
```
"The specialist team will be reaching out within [timeframe]. They have
all the information from our call today. Your ticket number is [number] -
please reference that when they call. Is there anything else before we
wrap up?"
```

### Close with Action Required from Customer
```
"Just to confirm next steps: you're going to [customer action], and then
contact us back at [when/condition]. Once we hear from you, we'll [next step].
Does that all make sense?"
```

## üí° USAGE NOTES

### Personalizing Templates
- **DO:** Adapt language to match your natural speaking style
- **DO:** Adjust technical level based on customer expertise
- **DO:** Add customer's name periodically for personal touch
- **DON'T:** Sound robotic or scripted
- **DON'T:** Use templates that don't fit the situation

### Cultural Considerations
- Be aware of cultural differences in communication style
- Some cultures prefer direct communication, others indirect
- Adjust formality based on customer cues
- When in doubt, err on side of professional politeness

### Tone Guidelines
- **Emergency:** Calm, directive, confident
- **Routine:** Friendly, helpful, patient
- **Angry Customer:** Empathetic, solution-focused, calm
- **Technical Customer:** Efficient, detailed, respectful of their knowledge

### What Not to Say
- ‚ùå "That's impossible" ‚Üí "That's unusual, let me investigate"
- ‚ùå "You must have done something wrong" ‚Üí "Let's see what happened"
- ‚ùå "I don't know" ‚Üí "Let me find that answer for you"
- ‚ùå "That's not my department" ‚Üí "I'll connect you with the right person"
- ‚ùå "You should have..." ‚Üí "For next time, I recommend..."
- ‚ùå "Just..." (minimizing) ‚Üí "You can..." (empowering)
